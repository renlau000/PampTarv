HISTORY

Pampanga, known as the Culinary Capital of the Philippines, is a culturally rich province in Central Luzon with a deep historical and religious heritage. During the Spanish colonial era, it was one of the most prosperous provinces, reflected in landmarks like the San Guillermo Church in Bacolor and the Baroque-inspired Betis Church in Guagua. Its natural beauty is equally notable, with Mount Arayat, the Pampanga River, and vast farmlands symbolizing its agricultural wealth. Festivals such as the Giant Lantern Festival and Sinukwan Festival celebrate Kapampangan creativity, mythology, and resilience, drawing visitors and keeping traditions alive.
